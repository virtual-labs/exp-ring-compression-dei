<h1>Ring Compression Test</h1>

<p>Ring compression test has been widely used to evaluate friction conditions in metal forming processes.</p>

<h2 class="section-title">THE PRINCIPLE OF RING TEST</h2>

<p>
    For given conditions of temperature, strain, strain rate and initial geometry (OD:ID:H),
    the deformation of internal diameter can be calculated as a function of friction coefficient.
</p>

<center><img src="images/RCT/RingDeformation.jpg" width="650" height="400"></center>
<br>

<h2 class="section-title">Geometrical Change and Friction Condition</h2>

<center><img src="images/RCT/GeometricalChange.jpg"></center>
<br>

<p>When a short ring specimen is compressed, internal diameter increases at low friction and decreases at high friction.</p>
<p>By measuring the change in ID, friction conditions can be evaluated using calibration curves.</p>

<span class="blue">For various forming conditions, the value of “m” varies as follows:</span>
<br><br>

<h2 class="section-title">COMPARISON</h2>
<br><br>

<center><img src="images/RCT/Comperision.jpg"></center>

<p>
A ring test simulation will be preformed for two different friction shear factors, one material, one press setup. The simulation is 
non-isothermal, therefore heat transfer at the interface is considered<br>
The simulations show how temperature and shear friction factor affect metal flow and ring geometry. Compared to isothermal, non-isothermal 
deformation causes the level of friction to increase.

</p>
